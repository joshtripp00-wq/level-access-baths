Stay in the Home You Love — Safely

The math on aging in place is straightforward: assisted living in the Omaha area routinely runs thousands of dollars a month, while one smart bathroom renovation costs less than a few months of it — and lets you stay in the home, neighborhood, and routines you've built a life around.

The bathroom is where to start because it's where the risk is. Slick surfaces, hard edges, a climb-over tub, nothing solid to hold — it's the most dangerous room in the house for older adults. It's also the most fixable.

Renovate once. Our aging-in-place installations are future-ready by default: reinforced blocking for grab bars anywhere, clearances that fit a walker later, controls that work seated or standing. One renovation now beats two renovations later.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an aging-in-place bathroom remodel?

It's a renovation designed so you can keep living safely and independently in your own home as your needs change — instead of moving to assisted living. The bathroom is where most aging-related home injuries happen, so it's where smart planning pays off most: barrier-free showering, secure grab bars, better lighting, and fixtures that work whether you're 62 or 92.

When is the right time to remodel for aging in place?

Before you need it. The best aging-in-place projects happen in your late 50s through 60s, when the renovation is a choice rather than an emergency after a fall or hospital discharge. Planning ahead means you get the design you want, on your schedule, at a normal pace — and you enjoy the safer bathroom for decades.

Does an aging-in-place remodel make my house look medical?

Not the way we do it. Solid-surface walls, modern matte-black or brushed-nickel fixtures, recessed niches, and built-in benches read as upscale design. Grab bars today come in finishes that match towel bars. Visitors see a beautiful bathroom; you know it's also engineered for the long run.

How much does an aging-in-place bathroom cost in Omaha?

Shower-focused projects start around $6,000 all-in, and broader scopes price according to the work involved. You get one comprehensive number at a free in-home consultation — and because we install reinforced blocking and plan for the future, you won't pay twice when needs change later.

What does 'future-ready' mean in your installations?

We build in capacity you may not need yet: reinforced blocking inside walls so grab bars can be added anywhere later without demolition, valve placements that work seated or standing, and clearances that accommodate a walker or chair down the road. It costs little now and saves a second renovation later.
